Waste Segregation Guide

Proper segregation of waste is important for a cleaner environment. Follow the guide below:

Step 1: Separate Your Waste

  • Biodegradable Waste - This includes kitchen waste, food scraps, and garden waste.
  • Non-Biodegradable Waste - Includes plastics, glass, metal, and other materials.
  • Recyclable Waste - Paper, cardboard, and certain plastics can be recycled.

Step 2: Use Separate Bins

Ensure you have different bins for biodegradable, non-biodegradable, and recyclable waste. Label the bins clearly.

Step 3: Dispose of Hazardous Waste Separately

Items like batteries, electronics, and chemicals need special handling. Look for local hazardous waste disposal centers.
